# <b>Factors Influencing Healthcare Costs for Patients </b>
## Main idea
Nowadays, private hospitals earn a lot because treatments are very expensive. I would like hospitals to lower treatment costs, but that is almost impossible because products, medicines, and medical equipment are expensive.

Therefore, I shifted my focus to finding ways to reduce treatment costs from the patient's perspective instead of asking hospitals to lower their prices. 

## Problem Statement
#### Can we reduce healthcare costs through early prevention and better healthcare decisions?
1. Some disease become too expensive when treated at later stages.
2. Lifestyle-related conditions can increase long-term healthcare spending.
3. Different healthcare access choices(emergency visits, clinics, private, gov) may lead to different costs
---
## Analytical Directions
### 1. Prevention vs Treatment Cost

Some diseases become significantly more expensive when they are treated at later stages rather than detected early.

#### Questions Explored
- How does late diagnosis affect treatment costs?
- Which diseases benefit the most from early detection?

---

### 2. Lifestyle and Healthcare Cost

Certain lifestyle factors contribute to chronic diseases that require long-term treatment and continuous medical care.

#### Questions Explored
- Do lifestyle factors influence long-term healthcare costs?
- Which diseases linked to lifestyle generate the highest healthcare expenses?

---

## 3. Healthcare Access and Cost

Healthcare costs may vary depending on where and how patients seek treatment, such as emergency departments, clinics, private hospitals, or government hospitals.

#### Questions Explored
- How do different healthcare services affect treatment costs?
- What healthcare choices could help patients reduce medical expenses?
